(Software) Development Engineer

A (Software) Development Engineer is the mastermind behind a computer program. They develop and build various software applications or platforms that meet users needs (What Does a Software Developer Do, n.d.). Furthermore, day-to-day tasks include building models or diagrams, testing software checking for bugs and inefficiencies, and the most important thing programming the required program. I think I am a good fit for this position as I am very interested in creating apps. In addition, I have experience in the following programming languages: HTML, CSS, Swift, and Python. One of the challenges I may encounter is the lack of expertise/knowledge to code a specific feature of the CafeProject. I can overcome my roadblock by researching online, watching tutorials, asking questions in the developer community (StackOverflow, Reddit, and GitHub).


QA Engineer

QA Engineer synonymous with Quality Assurance Engineer is where somebody is responsible for testing and overseeing the quality of the product that is being developed. This is a continuous process throughout the project in which he or she ensures that their program meets industry standards, verifies that it follows government regulations, and conducts through checks to make sure that there are no security vulnerabilities that may allow hackers to have access to sensitive information (How To Become a QA Engineer, 2021). Also, this field involves detailed documentation, audits, and management. Furthermore, they have to meet with a wide variety of people everyday, ranging from developers to directors, to understand their needs and requirements. I think I am a good fit for this job as I love testing other peoples’ programming and looking to see if they have any bugs or issues. The main obstacle that I may face here is that I don’t have much knowledge regarding governmental policies and laws which may hinder me from doing my job to the fullest extent. If laws put forth by the government are not followed correctly, they can have detrimental effects as team members could be sued for liability concerns. I can overcome this by reading books specific to this topic, taking an online course, or by consulting with an legal professional for advice.

Project Manager

A Project Manager is responsible for overseeing whole operations of the project and ensuring the tasks are being completed efficiently and effectively. They have to document the progress of their team members and ensure that project deadlines are being met. It is his/her responsibility for them to communicate frequently with the team to settle any roadblocks. I think I am an ok fit for this position because I am passionate about taking the initiative to lead projects but I don’t have good communication skills. I can overcome this problem by practicing presenting, and easing myself out of my comfort zone.

Product Manager

A Product Manager is someone who recognizes the user’s needs and the company’s overall objectives to deliver a product or service that aligns with their business goals. Furthermore, they must comprehend the current market, the needs and wants of the client, the specific technology that is being implemented in the project and the pros & cons of the program being designed to try to satisfy everyone as they will be the focal point for any questions or concerns that may arise in the future about the product. Daily tasks include building and managing product roadmaps, creating business plans for new products, and finding ways to adapt new technologies (White, 2021). I think I am not a good fit for this position because I am not good at making compromises that would make people from all sides happy. Also, this job is very stressful as it is a complex rule that requires a blend of soft and hard skills. In addition, everyday, I have to be involved with people from many departments and have to simultaneously do many different jobs at the same time. I can overcome this roadblock by being more socially interactive and easing myself out of my comfort zone. In addition, I can design a tracker that includes all the information such as comments, suggestions, and concerns from various people, so that I can effectively report back to my team.

Release Engineer

A Release Engineer works on a company’s implementation of its software products. For example, if the company is planning to release its mobile application, he/she would oversee building and publishing new version releases to the Google Play and Apple App Store. Furthermore, they ensure timely releases for their product and collaborate with their teammates to monitor existing product releases to ensure everything is running smoothly. In addition, a release engineer is responsible for improving the tools for continuous build, automated testing, and release management (What Does a Release Engineer Do? Role & Responsibilities, n.d.). Also, they have to ensure that their product is sustainable, and reliable to make sure that their product is operating smoothly. I think I am a good fit for this position because I have the capability to manage the release products and with my time management skills, I can make sure that everything is running on time. One of the hurdles I may face in the CafeProject is how the deployment of apps work to their respective app stores (Google Play Store for Android and Apple App Store for iOS). As mentioned previously, I can overcome this roadblock by researching online, watching YouTube videos on that topic, and taking online courses.
